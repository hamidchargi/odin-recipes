kosko recipe

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, grated or chopped
  • 500g chicken thighs (or lamb pieces, or skip for veggie)
  • 1 tbsp ginger, 1 tbsp pepper, 1 tsp turmeric, 1½ tsp salt
  • handful parsley + cilantro tied together
  • 1½ liter water (about 6 cups)

    • Veggies — use what you have, aim for 5–7

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas, soaked overnight (or 1 can, drained)
  • 2 carrots, peeled and halved
  • 2 turnips or 1 small potato, quartered
  • ¼ small cabbage, cut in wedges
  • 2 small zucchini, whole
  • wedge of pumpkin or squash
  • optional: handful raisins for sweetness

    • Couscous

  • 500g medium couscous (not instant if you can)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • about 2 cups hot water or broth

    • Method

    Heat oil in a deep pot or couscoussier base. Add onion, tomato, meat and spices. Cook 10 min uncovered, stirring, until it makes a thick sauce. Add chickpeas, herb bouquet and water. Bring to boil, cover, simmer 20 min. Add hard veg first: carrots, turnips, cabbage. Cook 10 min. Add soft veg: zucchini, pumpkin. Taste broth — it should be salty-peppery. Simmer another 15 min until everything is tender. If using canned chickpeas, add now. While broth cooks, make couscous: Put dry couscous in a wide bowl, drizzle 2 tbsp oil, rub with hands to coat. Sprinkle ½ cup hot water, mix, let sit 5 min, break lumps. Steam over the broth 15 min if you have a steamer basket. No steamer? Just pour 1½ cups boiling broth over couscous, cover tight 5 min, fluff with fork, add butter and salt, fluff again. To serve: mound couscous on a big plate, make a well in middle. Arrange meat in center, veggies around like a pyramid. Spoon hot broth over everything, keep extra broth in a bowl on side.
