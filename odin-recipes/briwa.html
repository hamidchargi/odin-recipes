recipe is

  • 500g blanched almonds — use half raw, half lightly fried in oil for deeper flavor
  • 180g powdered sugar
    • 40g unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 3 tbsp orange blossom water (ma zhar)
  • pinch of mastic or salt (optional, traditional)
  • pinch salt

    • 16 oz phyllo dough or warqa, cut into 3–4 cm wide strips ½ cup butter, melted for brushing 1 cup honey, warmed with 1–2 tbsp orange blossom water 2 cups neutral oil for frying

    Method

    Toast almonds until light tan and fragrant, cool. Grind to a fine but slightly sandy texture — not almond butter. Mix almonds with sugar, cinnamon, salt, softened butter and orange blossom water. Work into a paste you can roll. Shape into small balls or logs. Take one strip of warqa/phyllo, brush lightly with melted butter, place filling near the end, then fold in a triangle, right-over-left, like folding a flag, until sealed. Tuck the end with a dab of butter. This is the classic briouat fold. Heat oil to 170°C. Fry 2–3 minutes until golden, drain. Immediately dip hot briwat in warm honey (with orange blossom). Let sit 1 minute, then lift to a rack. Sprinkle sesame or crushed pistachio if you like. Keeps 2 weeks in airtight tin, or freeze before frying.
